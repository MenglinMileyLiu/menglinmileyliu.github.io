Menglin (Miley) Liu

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Political Science at the University of California, Davis, with research interests spanning American politics, urban governance, and methodology. My work investigates the impact of local political institutions, such as direct democracy and district-based elections, on public policy and governance outcomes.

Methodologically, I specialize in data mining, text analysis, and leveraging large language models (LLMs) to analyze unstructured data. I am the co-developer of the open-source Python package PoliPrompt, which streamlines text classification and scaling tasks for political science research. My ongoing projects include adapting visual language models (VLMs) for political content analysis and improving ordinal classification methods for scaling political concepts.

My research has been featured at conferences such as the American Political Science Association Annual Meeting and the Society for Political Methodology. I have also coauthored book chapters on crisis communication and articles exploring housing policies and political participation, which are currently under review in leading journals.

Beyond academia, I have experience as a Data and Policy Analyst for the Committee of 100, where I visualized legislative impacts and supported public policy initiatives. My interdisciplinary expertise bridges computational advancements with pressing questions in governance, public trust, and democratic accountability.
